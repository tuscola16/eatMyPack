# Energy Chews & Blocks: Nutrition Data for Trip Planning

## Overview

Chews and blocks fall between gels and bars. They deliver fast carbohydrates in small, chewable pieces, allow portion control (eat 1–3 pieces at a time), and avoid the "gel fatigue" some athletes experience. They require brief chewing, so they are slightly slower to consume than gels, but many athletes prefer the tactile sensation of chewing during long efforts.

## Calorie Density Comparison

| Product | Cal/oz |
|---|---|
| Clif Bloks | ~94.5 |
| GU Energy Chews | ~94.5 |
| Honey Stinger Chews | ~90.7 |

Chews are notably less calorie-dense than gels (~88–114 cal/oz for gels) and bars (~104–142 cal/oz) due to higher water content and chewable texture ingredients.

## Best Use Cases

| Scenario | Recommended | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Flavor fatigue from gels | Clif Bloks, Honey Stinger Chews | Chewing provides variety and psychological break |
| Precise micro-dosing | Clif Bloks (per block) | ~33 cal/block; eat exactly what you need |
| High sweat / hot conditions | Skratch Sport Chews | 310mg sodium per packet vs. 40–95mg for others |
| Caffeine management | Clif Bloks Black Cherry, Honey Stinger Caffeinated | Staggered caffeine delivery in small portions |
| Night running | Caffeinated chew variants | Portable, no mess, easy to eat while sleepy |

## Physical Notes

- **Clif Bloks**: 6 small cubes per packet; each cube ~10g; packet is roughly 3.5" × 2"; resealable zipper
- **Honey Stinger Chews**: 10 small round chews per packet; roughly jellybean-sized; non-resealable pouch
- **GU Chews**: 8 pieces per packet; slightly larger than Honey Stinger; non-resealable
- **Skratch Chews**: 4 larger chews per packet; fruit-leather texture
