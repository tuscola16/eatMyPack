# Drink Mixes & Hydration Powders: Nutrition Data for Trip Planning

## Overview

Drink mixes serve two overlapping purposes:
1. **Hydration** – replacing water and electrolytes lost in sweat
2. **Fueling** – delivering calories and carbohydrates alongside fluid

Products exist on a spectrum from pure electrolyte mixes (near-zero calories) to high-carb fueling powders (300–400+ cal/serving). The two ends of the spectrum serve very different needs and should not be substituted for each other without understanding the differences.

## Deep Dives

### Tailwind Endurance Fuel
- **Serving**: 1 scoop = 27g powder, 100 calories, 25g carbs, 303mg sodium
- **Standard use**: 2 scoops per 500 mL bottle = 200 cal, 50g carbs, 606mg sodium
- **Cal/oz powder**: ~105 cal/oz
- **Philosophy**: All-in-one solution — no need for separate gels or electrolytes on runs under ~8 hours
- **Best for**: Runners who prefer not to carry solid food; ultras where bottles are refilled at aid stations
- **Gut note**: Generally well-tolerated; some athletes find it too sweet after many hours

### Maurten 320
- **Serving**: 80g powder → 500 mL drink, 320 calories, 80g carbs
- **Cal/oz powder**: ~113.6 cal/oz
- **Philosophy**: Hydrogel forms in the stomach, reducing osmotic stress; designed for maximum carb absorption
- **Best for**: Elite athletes trained on high carb intake; time trials and fast ultras
- **Cost**: ~$3.50–$4.00 per serving; premium pricing
- **Gut note**: Widely praised as gentlest high-carb option; tasteless (unflavored by design)

### Skratch Labs Super High-Carb
- **Serving**: 105g powder, 400 calories, 100g carbs, 400mg sodium
- **Cal/oz powder**: ~120 cal/oz
- **Carb type**: Cluster dextrin (Highly Branched Cyclic Dextrin) — slower gastric emptying than maltodextrin
- **Best for**: Cyclists and ultra runners who can tolerate very high carb intake
- **Cost**: ~$5.25 per serving; most expensive option
- **Gut note**: Cluster dextrin is designed for minimal GI distress even at high doses

### Generation UCAN SuperStarch
- **Serving**: 25g powder, ~92–110 calories, 26g carbs
- **Philosophy**: Slow-digesting corn starch → steady blood glucose without insulin spikes
- **Best for**: Low-to-moderate intensity efforts; fat adaptation training; athletes with blood sugar sensitivity
- **Limitation**: Not ideal for very high-intensity racing (needs faster carbs)
- **Gut note**: Generally gentle; very low osmolality

### Precision Hydration PH Series
- **Three tiers**: PH 250 (250mg Na), PH 500, PH 1000
- **Purpose**: Electrolyte replacement calibrated to individual sweat rate; not a calorie source
- **Best used with**: Separate carb sources (gels, bars, Tailwind)
- **Sweat test**: PH offers a free sweat test to determine individual sodium needs

| Precision Hydration | ~100–113 |

> Note: These are calories per ounce of dry powder carried. Since you add water on course, the "carried weight" calorie density of powders is much higher than prepared drinks.

## Planning Calculator Notes

For app integration:
- **Tailwind**: User carries powder; add water at aid stations. Each scoop (27g) = 100 cal. At 2 scoops/bottle, 54g of powder per 600mL bottle.
- **Maurten 320**: One sachet (80g) per 500mL bottle.
- **Skratch Hydration**: One packet (21g) per 500mL bottle = 80 cal.
- **Nuun**: 1 tablet per 500mL bottle = ~15 cal (essentially zero fuel value; pure hydration).
