# Freeze-Dried & Dehydrated Backpacking Meals: Nutrition Data

## Overview

Freeze-dried meals are the standard for multi-day backpacking. They are:
- Extremely lightweight (typically 100–170g dry)
- Long shelf life (25–30 years for Mountain House)
- High calorie density for dry weight (100–145 cal/oz dry)
- Require only boiling water (or cold soak with longer wait)
- Not typically used for ultra running (too bulky and time-consuming)

**Key distinction**: Calorie density is measured on **dry weight** (what you carry). After adding water, calorie density drops dramatically (roughly 1/4 of dry density).

---

## Major Brands Overview

### Mountain House
- Most popular brand; widest distribution
- Generally highest calorie density per dry ounce among freeze-dried brands
- 25-year shelf life
- Good flavor consistency
- Available at REI, Amazon, outdoor retailers
- **Target**: ~100–145 cal/oz dry

### Backpacker's Pantry
- Strong variety; competitive calories
- Often lower cost per calorie than Mountain House
- Higher proportion of organic/whole food options
- Some single-serve vs. two-serve pouches
- **Target**: ~95–135 cal/oz dry

### Good To-Go
- Premium positioning; chef-developed recipes
- Real ingredient quality
- Lower calorie density than Mountain House (more vegetables)
- **Target**: ~85–115 cal/oz dry

### Peak Refuel
- Newer brand; competitive density
- Marketing to hunters and military
- **Target**: ~110–145 cal/oz dry

### Patagonia Provisions
- Sustainability-focused
- Lower calorie density; less weight-optimized
- Premium price
- **Target**: ~90–110 cal/oz dry

## Freeze-Dried Meal Planning Numbers

**Standard assumptions for trip planning:**
- Average meal: ~500–600 cal (two servings from one pouch)
- Dry weight per meal: ~100–150g (~3.5–5.3 oz)
- Target cal/oz: 100–145 cal/oz dry
- Water needed: 1.5–2.5 cups boiling water per pouch; wait 8–12 minutes

**Weight budget example (per person, per day):**
- 3 meals × 120g average = 360g (12.7 oz) of food
- Total calories from meals: ~1,500–1,800 cal
- Remaining ~2,000–3,500 cal from snacks/bars/gels

## Cold Soak Option

Most freeze-dried meals can be cold-soaked (no stove needed):
- Add cold water; wait 20–45 minutes
- Texture is softer/mushier but nutritionally identical
- Saves stove fuel weight (~2–4 oz for a canister)
- Popular technique for ultralight backpackers
