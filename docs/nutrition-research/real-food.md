# Real Food for Ultra Running & Backpacking

## Overview

Real food becomes increasingly important after 4–6 hours of running. Athletes naturally shift away from sweet, processed gels toward savory, fat-rich, and solid foods. This is driven by:
- Palatability burnout from repeated sweet flavors
- Increasing fat oxidation at lower intensities (later in race)
- Psychological need for "normal" eating
- Better satiety and longer-lasting energy from mixed macros

Research from 100-mile races shows finishers consumed approximately **5x more fat** than non-finishers, largely from cheese, nut butters, hummus, jerky, and savory snacks.

## Calorie Density Quick Reference

| Cal/oz Range | Foods |
|---|---|
| Very high (130+ cal/oz) | Peanut butter, almond butter, chips, bacon, nuts |
| High (100–130 cal/oz) | Cheese, tortillas (dry), Saltines, pretzels |
| Moderate (70–100 cal/oz) | PB&J, quesadilla, bread, grilled cheese, gummy bears |
| Low (40–70 cal/oz) | Mashed potatoes, rice balls, pancakes, hard-boiled eggs |
| Very low (<40 cal/oz) | Cooked potatoes, cooked rice, ramen, fruit, broth |

## Rice Balls (Onigiri) - Elite Runner Favorite

Rice balls are extremely popular with elite ultra runners, especially those influenced by Japanese trail running culture. They are:
- Compact: ~100–120g per ball
- ~150–180 calories with fillings (plum, salmon, sesame)
- Cal/oz: ~42–51
- Gut-friendly: plain white rice is among the most easily digested carbs
- Can be made ahead and wrapped in plastic; good for 6–8 hours unrefrigerated
- Kilian Jornet has referenced rice-based fueling in interviews

## DIY Aid Station / Drop Bag Strategy

**Best foods for drop bags (no refrigeration, 12+ hours):**
- Peanut butter packets (Justin's, RxBar nut butter)
- Flour tortillas + individual peanut butter packets
- Gummy bears / candy
- Chips (individual bags)
- Saltine crackers
- Pretzels
- Energy bars (Larabar, Clif, Honey Stinger)
- Fruit (banana, orange slices — must be consumed same day)

**Best foods for crew-accessible aid stations:**
- Boiled salted potatoes (warm)
- Broth / ramen (hot thermos)
- PB&J cut into halves or quarters
- Quesadillas
- Pizza slices (cold is fine)
- Pancakes / waffles (morning races)

## Digestibility Tiers for Real Food

### Tier 1: Eat anytime (even at high intensity)
- Banana
- White rice / rice ball
- Boiled potatoes (salted)
- Gummy bears / candy
- Broth
- Saltines / pretzels

### Tier 2: Best at moderate pace or aid station
- PB&J half sandwich
- Quesadilla
- Grilled cheese
- Rice ball with filling
- Chips

### Tier 3: Aid station only / low intensity
- Full sandwich
- Mac & cheese / ramen
- Pizza
- Bacon
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Cheese + crackers
