# Energy Gels: Nutrition Data for Trip Planning

## Overview

Energy gels are the most weight-efficient carbohydrate delivery format for endurance athletes. They are semi-liquid, require no chewing, and digest rapidly. Most gels rely on maltodextrin and/or fructose as primary carbohydrate sources.

**Key note on Spring Energy:** In 2022–2023, iRunFar and independent lab tests confirmed that Spring Energy's Awesome Sauce gel contained fewer than half the labeled calories (75 kcal actual vs. 180 kcal claimed; 18g carbs vs. 45g claimed). Other Spring products may also be mislabeled. Spring Energy data below reflects labeled values but should be treated as unreliable until verified.

## Best Use Cases by Race Stage

| Stage | Recommended Gels | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Early miles / high intensity | GU Original, GU Roctane, Maurten 100/160, SiS Beta Fuel | Easy to consume, fast absorbing |
| Mid-race (steady pace) | Maurten 100, Honey Stinger, Clif Shot | Consistent fueling; familiar flavors |
| Late miles / nausea risk | SiS Go Isotonic, Maurten 100, Huma (real food base) | Gentle on stomach; easy to get down |
| Maximum carb loading | Maurten 160, SiS Beta Fuel, PF 90 | High carb per packet; fewer stops needed |
| Weight-critical efforts | GU Roctane, GU Original, Honey Stinger Gel | Best cal/g ratio among standard gels |

## Volume Estimates

Standard gel packets are approximately:
- 32–44g gels (GU, Huma, Honey Stinger): roughly 1.5–2 fl oz volume; fit in vest pockets; palm-sized
- 60g gels (SiS): slightly longer tube; ~2.5 fl oz
- 153g PF 90: large resealable pouch; ~5 fl oz; designed to portion out over time
- Maurten 40g: small flat sachet; compact
