# Nut Butters & Squeeze Packs: Nutrition Data for Trip Planning

## Overview

Nut butter squeeze packs are a high-calorie, high-fat, moderate-protein option popular with both ultra runners and backpackers. They:
- Deliver ~160–210 calories per 32g packet
- Provide fat + protein for sustained energy (not just fast carbs)
- Are shelf-stable for months
- Require no preparation
- Can be eaten directly from packet, spread on a tortilla, or squeezed into oatmeal

Cal/oz for nut butters (~160–175 cal/oz) is among the highest of any portable food, second only to oils.

## Use Cases

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Running (mid-effort) | Justin's or RxBar single serve; eat directly from packet; pair with gel for carbs |
| Running (aid station) | PB on bread, tortilla, or crackers; higher-carb version of nut butter fueling |
| Backpacking snack | Justin's packet + tortilla or crackers |
| Calorie-dense backpacking staple | Jar of peanut or almond butter; eat by the spoon or spread on tortilla |
| Protein boost (late race / recovery) | RxBar nut butter (9g protein per packet) |
| Nausea / sweet fatigue | Savory peanut butter (Justin's Classic); breaks sweet monotony |

## Gut Friendliness

Nut butters are **moderate** in digestibility during exercise:
- **High fat content** slows gastric emptying — good for sustained energy but can cause nausea at high effort
- **Best consumed**: at aid stations, walking sections, or during lower-intensity hiking
- **Not recommended**: during high-intensity running or racing (stomach not handling fat well)
- **Exception**: small amounts (half a packet) are generally fine even while moving

---

## Physical Notes

- Justin's 1.15 oz packet: ~3" × 2" flat foil packet; tear top open; very packable
- RxBar nut butter: similar flat foil format; 1.13 oz
- A 16 oz jar of peanut butter: ~1,800 calories; weighs 454g (1 lb); ~2.5 days of snack supply for one person
- For ultralight backpacking: individual packets add ~2–3g of wrapper waste vs. jar
