# Energy Bars: Nutrition Data for Trip Planning

## Overview

Energy bars are a staple for both ultra runners and backpackers. They offer more bulk and satiety than gels, include protein and fat alongside carbohydrates, and are easier to stomach during lower-intensity efforts or at aid stations. Bars vary widely in calorie density: simple oat/date bars run ~100–130 cal/oz while nut-heavy bars can approach 140+ cal/oz.

| Honey Stinger Waffle | Excellent | Simple carbs; very easy to digest while moving |
| Larabar | Very Good | Dates + nuts; real food; minimal processing |

## Best Use Cases

| Scenario | Recommended Bars | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Running / during effort | Honey Stinger Waffle, Larabar, Picky Bar | Easy chewing; quick-digesting |
| Aid station / walking break | Clif Bar, RxBar, ProBar, Bobo's | Can eat more substantial food when not hammering |
| Backpacking (calorie density) | Honey Stinger Waffle, Kind Nut, Larabar | High cal/oz for pack weight goals |
| Backpacking (satiety / meal replacement) | ProBar Meal, Bobo's, Clif Builder's | Larger bars; more filling |
| Savory craving relief | Kind Nuts & Sea Salt, RxBar | Lower sweetness than other bars |
